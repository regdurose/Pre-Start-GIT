No

Item

Action

2 MR AND DFR Complete the relevant paper work
3 FUEL QUANTITY Note the remaining fuel quantity and calculate the next fuel load to inform the refueller

3. Cover the instrument console

IT IS NOT MANDATORY BUT A PREFFERED PROCEDURE. IF RESISTENCE IS FELT WHILE MANUALLY TURNING THE BLADES DO NOT FORCE IT BUT SEEK MAINTENANCE ASSISTENCE

ALTIMETER CROSS CHECK

Two (2) altimeters installed on IFR aircraft with current QNH set
If both altimeters show 60ft or less difference from the Aerodrome Elevation then OK for IFR flight
If one altimeter shows greater than 60ft difference but less than 75ft then OK for the first leg only for IFR flight.
If on the second leg both altimeters do not show 60ft or less difference from the Aerodrome Elevation then then IFR flight is not permitted
If either altimeter shows greater than 75ft difference from the Aerodrome Elevation the altimeter is unserviceable and IFR flight is not permitted

NAVIGATING BETWEEN RADIALS. POINT TO POINT

1 - Base it on radials you are actually on and want to go to not headings
2 - Mark radial and distance from station to commence turn
3 - Mark radial to intercept and its distance from station
4 - Rotate bezel so that marks line up vertically from your position to where you want to go
5 - Read off heading to steer at top of index and count distance between the two positions
6 - Convert the distance to a time based on GS
7 - Make allowance for wind
