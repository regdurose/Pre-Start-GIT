Post Flight Inspection

Post Flight Inspection

No

Item

Action
1 Main blades still turning Wait in the helicopter until the main rotor blades have completely stopped turning

WARNING: ATTEMPTING TO CATCH THE MAIN ROTOR BLADE WHILE IT IS STILL TURNING CAN CAUSE INJURY TO PERSONNEL AND DAMAGE TO THE AIRCRAFT

2 MR and DFR Complete the relevant paper work
3 Fuel quantity Note the remaining fuel quantity and calculate the next fuel load to inform the refueller
4 Exit the helicopter

NOTE: IT IS PREFERABLE TO TURN THE MAIN ROTOR BLADES BACKWARDS WHEN TYING THEM DOWN. THIS IS TO HELP ELEVIATE TURBINE RUB OR CARBON BUILD UP ON THE LABYRINTH SEAL. IT IS NOT MANDATORY BUT A PREFFERED PROCEDURE. IF RESISTENCE IS FELT WHILE MANUALLY TURNING THE BLADES DO NOT FORCE IT BUT SEEK MAINTENANCE ASSISTENCE.
